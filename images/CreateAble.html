CreateAble aims to empower people with physical disabilities by organizing assistive-device build-a-thons hosted at local Makerspaces, Hackerspaces, and Community Spaces.

With funding and support from the Oceanpath Fellowship and the Coady International Institute, CreateAble will host multiple events in cities accross Canada over 2016 and 2017. We're looking for local advocates, makerspaces, and disability organizations to partner with.



CreateAble connects people with disabilities to existing Makerspaces to create or repair their own assistive devices:

What's a Makerspace?

Makerspaces are semi-public workshops where communities can come together to use tools ranging from digital tools (3D Printers, CNC mills, and laser cutters) to traditional woodworking and metalworking tools.



Makerspaces have emerged in cities across Canada:



These spaces have strong communities of makers and a wide variety of tools at their disposal - the main idea behind CreateAble is to connect these communities and tools to persons with disabilities, who have a huge need for custom-made assistive devices.

Assistive Devices

Assistive devices promote greater independence for people with disabilitites by enabling peoeple to perform tasks that they were formerly unable to accomplish, or had great difficulty accomplishing, by providing enhancements to, or changing methods of interacting with, the technoloy needed to accomplish such tasks.

Assistive Devices

Assistive devices range from wheelchairs, to custom grips, to systems that enable use of computers. These devices don't have to be complex or expensive; they just have to be reliable and well-suited to the person's needs.
